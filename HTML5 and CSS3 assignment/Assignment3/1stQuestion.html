C. V. Raman

Chandrasekhara Venkata Raman was an Indian physicist who made groundbreaking works in the field of light scattering. He was the recipient of the Nobel Prize for Physics in 1930 for the discovery that when light traverses a transparent material, some of the light that is deflected changes in wavelength. This phenomenon is now called Raman scattering and is the result of the Raman effect.

Physcist (1888-1970)

Timeline

  • 1888: Born in Tiruchirapalli, Madras, British India.
  • 1904: Topped Bachelor's degree examination in Physics.
  • 1906: Published first research paper on Diffraction of Light in British journal Philosophical Magazine.
  • 1907: Completed M.A. degree with highest distinction form University of Madras.
  • 1907: Qualified for the Indian Finance Service and appointed as Assistant Accountant General.
  • 1917: He resigned as civil servant and became orofessor of physics at University of Calcutta.
  • 1926: Established Indian Journal of Physics.
  • 1928: Published first observations of Raman Effect.
  • 1930: Won the Nobel Prize in Physics for his work on the scattering light and discovery of Raman effect.
  • 1954: He was awarded the Bharat Ratna whic is a Highest Civilian award of India.
  • 1970: Died in Banglore, Mysore state, India.
